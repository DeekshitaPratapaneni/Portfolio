Starbucks Consumer Analytics: Understanding Market Position & Customer Segmentation

Problem Statement: With increasing competition in the coffeehouse industry, Starbucks needs to understand its market position, customer preferences, and brand perception to enhance its competitive advantage. This project analyzes consumer behavior, segmentation, and positioning to determine whether Starbucks needs a repositioning strategy and how it can optimize its marketing efforts to attract more customers

Actions Taken: A consumer survey was conducted to collect demographic and behavioral data, including age, income, dining frequency, and brand loyalty. Segmentation analysis categorized customers into three groups:

  • Steadfast Customers (loyal and social)
  • Incentivized Customers (influenced by discounts and loyalty programs)
  • Torpid Customers (not loyal and not discount-driven)
Discriminant analysis was performed to identify key factors influencing brand perception. A positioning analysis mapped Starbucks' brand perception relative to competitors like Dutch Bros, identifying strengths and areas for improvement

Results: Starbucks is perceived as a prestigious brand with great customer service and a social hub, but it struggles with value-for-money perception compared to McCafé. Taste, quality, accessibility, and customer service are the most influential attributes for loyal customers. While Starbucks does not require a major repositioning, expanding its food variety can help prevent competition from McCafé, Dutch Bros and others. Market expansion into packaged coffee and iced beverages and tailoring menus to local tastes can further strengthen its presence
