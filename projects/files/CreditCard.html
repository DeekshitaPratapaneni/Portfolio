Walmart Sales Analysis: Predictive Analytics

Problem Statement: Walmart, one of the largest multinational retail corporations, operates thousands of stores worldwide. To optimize its supply chain, improve sales forecasting, and enhance decision-making, Walmart requires an in-depth analysis of historical sales data. This project analyzes weekly sales data from 45 different stores, identifying key trends, seasonality, and external factors affecting sales performance.

Actions Taken: The weekly sales data (2010-2012) for 45 Walmart stores was cleaned and standardized, ensuring accuracy and consistency. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) identified key trends, revealing higher sales in December, better performance in moderate temperatures, and lower sales during holidays, high inflation, and unemployment extremes. Top-performing stores and low-performing stores were identified. Using Linear Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost, sales forecasting models were developed, with Random Forest achieving the highest accuracy, reducing WMAE by 64%. Results and Key Insights: Sales peak in December, highlighting strong seasonality, while moderate temperatures drive better performance. Higher unemployment negatively impacts sales, suggesting targeted promotions during economic downturns. Holidays affect sales unpredictably, with some boosting revenue and others causing declines. Moderate fuel prices correlate with higher sales, and top-performing stores can serve as benchmarks to improve low-performing locations
