Netflix Content Analysis

Problem Statement: Netflix, a global streaming platform, continuously expands its content library to attract and retain subscribers. Understanding viewer preferences, content engagement, and streaming patterns is crucial for optimizing recommendations, improving retention, and increasing revenue. This project analyzes content performance, peak streaming hours, and user behavior trends to provide data-driven insights that enhance user experience and business strategy

Actions Taken: The dataset was cleaned and standardized to ensure accuracy, with missing values handled and categorical variables transformed. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) revealed popular genres, peak viewing times, and user engagement trends, highlighting which content drives the most watch hours. Machine learning models were implemented to analyze factors influencing content success, focusing on genre performance, regional preferences, and subscription impact

Results and Key Insights: Netflix engagement peaks during evenings and weekends, with action, drama, and thriller genres leading in watch hours. Personalized recommendations significantly improve retention, encouraging longer watch times. New releases attract subscriptions, but long-term engagement depends on content quality and user preferences. Regional variations show that localized content boosts viewership, emphasizing the need for strategic content acquisitions
